Expert Advice

PRO TIPS

⚠️

Warm Up First

Shoulders are the most injury-prone joint. Always do 2 sets of face pulls and band rotations before pressing.

📐

Don't Go Too Wide

On lateral raises, stop at shoulder height. Going higher shifts load to traps and impinges the shoulder joint.

🔄

Train All 3 Heads

Front, side, and rear deltoids. Most people only train front and side — the rear delt is what makes shoulders look 3D.

💡

Light Is Enough

Lateral raises with 5–8kg and perfect form beat 20kg with momentum. Slow down, feel the burn in the right place.
